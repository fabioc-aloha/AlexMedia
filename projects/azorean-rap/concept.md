# Concept: Azorean Rap — Local Artists & Culture

**Project:** Research and media production exploring the rap scene in the Azores
**Focus:** Sandro G. and other local Azorean rap artists
**Goal:** Document the artists, their music, lyrical themes, and the cultural context of rap in the Azores

## Research Questions

1. Who are the active rap artists from the Azores?
2. What themes define Azorean rap — island identity, emigration, saudade, local dialect?
3. How does the Azorean rap scene differ from mainland Portuguese rap?
4. What role does Azorean Portuguese (dialect, slang) play in the lyrics?
5. What platforms and venues support the local scene?
6. How do artists balance local identity with broader Portuguese/international appeal?

## Cultural Context

### The Azores & Music

- Nine volcanic islands in the mid-Atlantic, autonomous region of Portugal
- Strong traditions in folk music (cantigas, chamarrita, fado variations)
- Diaspora connections to the US (Massachusetts, Rhode Island, California) and Canada
- Rap scene emerging as younger generations blend island identity with global hip-hop culture

### Themes to Explore

- **Insularity** — life on a remote island, limited opportunities, ocean as both prison and freedom
- **Emigration** — the pull of the mainland and diaspora communities
- **Identity** — being Azorean vs. Portuguese vs. global
- **Language** — local dialect, code-switching between Azorean Portuguese and standard Portuguese
- **Nature** — volcanic landscapes, ocean, weather as lyrical imagery
