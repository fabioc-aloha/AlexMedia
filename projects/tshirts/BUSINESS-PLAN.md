# T-Shirt & Poster Print-on-Demand Business Plan

## Executive Summary

AI-generated t-shirts and posters sold through print-on-demand (POD) storefronts. Zero inventory, zero upfront cost per design, near-zero marginal cost to create new designs (AI generation at $0.02-0.10/image). Revenue comes from the markup between POD base cost and retail price.

**Business model**: Design → Upload → Customer orders → POD provider prints & ships → You keep the margin.

**Competitive edge**: We already have the full generation pipeline (`generate-image.js` with 14+ AI models) and the API integration (`generate-tshirt.js` for Printify/Printful). Most POD sellers create designs manually or pay designers $20-100 per design. We generate them for pennies in seconds.

**Industry note (Jan 2025)**: Printful and Printify merged to form **Fyul**, combining catalogs and 10M+ seller reach. Both APIs remain operational under the Fyul umbrella. This consolidation may affect pricing and policy long-term — diversify across providers.

## Pros & Cons

### Etsy

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Huge built-in audience of buyers looking for unique items | Fees stack up: listing + transaction + processing + offsite ads |
| Strong SEO — Etsy listings rank on Google | Offsite ads fee mandatory for ALL sellers (15% <$10K, 12% ≥$10K) |
| Printify/Printful integrate directly | Competitive — millions of sellers in the t-shirt niche |
| Trusted platform, buyer confidence | Etsy owns the customer relationship |
| Good for "AI-generated" as a selling point (novelty) | AI-generated designs face some community pushback |

### Redbubble

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Zero fees, zero risk — pure profit on every sale | Low margins (default ~20% markup = ~$4/shirt) |
| They handle everything (print, ship, returns, CS) | No control over pricing, shipping, or quality |
| Upload once, sell on 70+ product types automatically | Heavily saturated marketplace |
| Passive income — upload and forget | No customer data or email list |
| Great for posters and wall art specifically | Slow payments (monthly, 2-month delay) |

### Amazon Merch

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Massive audience, Amazon trust factor | Invite-only, slow tier progression |
| Zero cost, zero risk | Very low royalties (~$2-5/sale) |
| Amazon handles everything including customer service | Extremely competitive |
| Prime shipping attracts buyers | Strict content policy — AI designs may face review |
| No inventory management | You own nothing — Amazon can shut you down |

### Shopify + POD

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Full brand control and ownership | You must drive your own traffic |
| Build an email list and customer relationships | $39/mo fixed cost before any sales |
| Highest profit margins (no marketplace fees) | Requires marketing budget and effort |
| Professional storefront, custom domain | More setup work than marketplaces |
| Scale without limits | Customer trust harder to establish initially |

### Spring (TeeSpring)

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Creator-focused with YouTube/Twitch integration | Small marketplace, limited organic traffic |
| Free, you set your own markup | Lower quality perception vs. Printful |
| Good for content creator merch | Limited product customization |
| Social commerce features | Less buyer trust than Etsy/Amazon |

### Gelato

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| 140+ hubs in 32 countries — true global local production | Smaller product catalog than Printify |
| 97% of orders delivered in 72 hours | Fewer customization options for apparel |
| Sustainability leader (lowest carbon per order) | No built-in marketplace (need own storefront) |
| No commission, no transaction fees | Premium features require Gelato+ subscription |
| 4.8/5 Shopify rating (800+ reviews) | Home décor/stationery focus more than apparel |

### Gooten

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| 200+ products, strong home décor selection | Only 3.5/5 Shopify rating — mixed support reviews |
| Flat-rate shipping simplifies pricing | Less brand recognition vs Printful/Printify |
| 50+ manufacturers across 70+ regions | Quality varies by manufacturer in network |
| Shopify Certified App Partner | No built-in marketplace |

## Recommended Strategy

### Phase 1: Launch (Week 1-2)

1. **Platform**: Start with **Etsy + Printify** (built-in audience, low barrier)
2. **Simultaneously**: Upload to **Redbubble** (zero cost, passive income)
3. **Products**: T-shirts + posters (both use same design, different POD products)
4. **Initial catalog**: 20-30 designs across 3-4 niches
5. **Our collections**: "AI Created Me This T-Shirt" line + 2 more niche collections

### Phase 2: Optimize (Month 1-3)

1. Track which designs sell — double down on winners
2. Expand winning niches to 50+ designs each
3. Optimize Etsy SEO (titles, tags, descriptions)
4. Generate seasonal designs ahead of holidays
5. Price test: try $22.99, $24.99, $27.99 to find sweet spot

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 3-6)

1. Add **Shopify store** for highest margins once you have traffic
2. Build email list from Etsy + social media
3. Launch **Amazon Merch** (apply early, tier progression is slow)
4. Expand to mugs, hoodies, canvas prints, phone cases
5. Create design "collections" (matching aesthetic themes)

### Phase 4: Brand (Month 6+)

1. Establish brand identity — not just random designs, a curated collection
2. Social media presence (Instagram, TikTok showing AI design process)
3. Content marketing — "Watch AI design a t-shirt in 10 seconds" videos
4. Affiliate program through Shopify
5. Wholesale inquiries for bulk orders

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Market saturation | Medium | Focus on specific niches, not generic designs |
| AI design backlash | Low | Lean into it — "AI Created Me" is the brand |
| Platform policy changes | High | Multi-platform presence reduces dependency |
| Fyul (Printful+Printify) consolidation | Medium | Diversify across Gelato, Gooten, SPOD. Monitor fee changes post-merger |
| Copyright/trademark | High | Never use copyrighted characters, brands, or likenesses |
